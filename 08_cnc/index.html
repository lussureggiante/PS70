Voila! ‚ú® ![image of embroidery](./cnc1.gif) ![image of embroidery](./cnc2.jpg) <br> ##### üíç Assignment 8.2 Description Create something by molding and casting. <br> ##### üíç Process In class, Nathan mentioned that jewelry could be a good option for molding and casting, yet there haven't been many students worked on it. So I decided to making cement rings. I first modeled 2 rings in Fusion360 and 3D printed them. Fastened each ring to a craft stick with a screw, and also added a straw as an opening for the air to flow in later stage when the casting material will be poured into the mold. I then taped everything to small paper cups, and mixed silicon rubber to create the molds. The next day, I came back to the lab and the molds were cured. I carefully dismantled the accessories. It was delicate work especially when it comes to cutting the hollowed circle in the middle of the ring, but things went well. I then mixed gypsum cement and poured it into the molds in the cups. The rings were small, the air holes were small, the cement had high viscosity, it was a messy struggle ü´†. Another day passed, I came back again and found the casting to be a success! The material was soft and sanding was easy. I then decided to melt some metal and cast 2 more metal rings with the same molds. The process was a lot faster as it only took seconds for the metal to set in room temperature, and they turned out to be really cute too. I also love the random texture on the ring created by the excessive metal.
