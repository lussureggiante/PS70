<br> #### <b> Week 2: Laser Cutting - Making a Lantern (and Some 3D Modeling)</b> <br> ##### 🏮 Assignment 2.1 Description Create a press-fit construction kit that can be assembled in multiple ways, and accounts for lasercutter kerf. Expect to make multiple trips to the laser cutter, as pieces often won't fit together perfectly the first time. <br> ##### 🏮 Inspiration The idea of making a lantern came from the traditional Lantern Festival which fell on last Sunday (Feb 5th). Additional aesthetic references were from Olafur Eliasson's installations. ![image of embroidery](./inspo1.jpeg) ![image of embroidery](./inspo2.jpg) <br> ##### 🏮 Design Here are a few renders to illustrate the design I have in mind. The ideal material for this lantern would be arylic sheets. ![image of embroidery](./render1.jpg) ![image of embroidery](./render2.jpg) These elements can be assembled in various ways to form interesting shapes. ![image of embroidery](./render3.jpg) ![image of embroidery](./render4.jpg) <br> ##### 🏮 Modeling I made 3d models of the components needed to assemble a simple lantern in Fusion 360, and projected 2d drawings for laser cutting. The dimensions were adjusted based on the measurement of the cardboard that would be used in actual fabrication. ![image of embroidery](./fusion1.png) ![image of embroidery](./laser1.png) <br> ##### 🏮 Laser Cutting & Assembly Viola! ✨ ![image of embroidery](./laser2.png) ![image of embroidery](./laser3.jpg) ![image of embroidery](./laser5.jpg) ![image of embroidery](./laser6.jpg) <br> <br> ##### 📐 Assignment 2.2 & 2.3 Description Work through a Fusion 360 tutorial and document what you produce. Select at least 2 household objects or components from the lab (could be items you think you might include in your final project). Measure them using calipers, and model them in Fusion 360 (or other program). <br> ##### 📐 Learning & Using Fusion 360 I followed <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SzSaIl_-68g" target="_blank"> this tutorial</a> to learn to create a bended object, and made a model of a fork. Then I found 2 household items that I thought would be interesting to model: a wine bottle and a sunblock tube. ![image of embroidery](./fork1.png) ![image of embroidery](./fork2.png) ![image of embroidery](./fork3.jpg) ![image of embroidery](./wine1.png) ![image of embroidery](./wine2.png) ![image of embroidery](./wine3.jpg) ![image of embroidery](./tube1.png) ![image of embroidery](./tube2.png) ![image of embroidery](./tube3.jpg) <br> And... a reveal of how they actually look like in real life on my desk. ✨ ![image of embroidery](./wine.jpg) ![image of embroidery](./tube.jpg) <br> //END OF ASSIGNMENT 2// <br> Click <a href="https://olafureliasson.net/" target="_blank"> here</a> to see more works by Olafur Eliasson, one of my favorite artists. <br>
