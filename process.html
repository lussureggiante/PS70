Specifically, how to make sure the vibration motors will stay close to the neck, while the users are able to move freely. My original idea was to 3D print a supporting structure, but realized it was not flexible enough, and it took really long. In the end, I decided to go with laser cutting acrylic sheet to make the structure, and living hinge helped with the flexibility needed. For the haptic feedback around the head, I used ecoflex-0030 to cast 2 layers of soft silicone sheets, and combined them together with a soft tube I bought from Adafruit. The molds were made from laser cutting acrylic sheets too. It works in the same way as the soft robotics we learnt from the class.
